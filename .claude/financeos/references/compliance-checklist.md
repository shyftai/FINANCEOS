## Revenue Recognition (ASC 606)

### Five-Step Model

| Step | Action | SaaS Application |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| 1. Identify contract | Signed agreement or accepted terms | Subscription agreement, click-through |
| 2. Identify performance obligations | Distinct goods/services promised | Software access, support, implementation |
| 3. Determine transaction price | Total consideration expected | Monthly/annual subscription price |
| 4. Allocate to obligations | Standalone selling price | Allocate across bundled services |
| 5. Recognize when satisfied | Over time or at a point in time | Subscription: ratably over term |

### Common SaaS Scenarios

| Scenario | Recognition Method |
|----------|-------------------|
| Monthly subscription | Recognize monthly as service delivered |
| Annual prepaid subscription | Recognize 1/12 per month (deferred revenue) |
| Implementation/setup fee | Over implementation period if distinct |
| Usage-based billing | Recognize as usage occurs |
| Multi-year deal with discount | Allocate total price ratably |

## Tax Compliance

### Sales Tax

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Nexus determination | Track physical presence and economic nexus by state |
| Registration | Register in all nexus states |
| Collection | Charge correct rate based on customer location |
| Remittance | File and pay by state deadline |
| Exemption certificates | Collect and store for exempt customers |
| SaaS taxability | Varies by state — some exempt, some taxable |

### Payroll Compliance

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Federal withholding | W-4 on file for all employees |
| State withholding | Based on employee work location |
| FICA (Social Security + Medicare) | 7.65% employer match |
| FUTA | 6% on first $7,000 (credit for state payments) |
| SUTA | Varies by state and experience rating |
| Workers' compensation | Required in most states |
| New hire reporting | Report within 20 days (varies by state) |

## Audit Preparation

### Documents to Have Ready

| Category | Documents |
|----------|----------|
| **Corporate** | Articles of incorporation, bylaws, board minutes, cap table |
| **Financial** | Trial balance, GL detail, bank statements, reconciliations |
| **Revenue** | Customer contracts, revenue schedule, deferred revenue roll-forward |
| **Expenses** | Vendor contracts, AP aging, expense reports, credit card statements |
| **Payroll** | Payroll registers, benefit plans, contractor agreements, 1099s |
| **Tax** | Prior year returns, estimated payments, nexus analysis |
| **Equity** | Stock option plan, 409A valuation, grant agreements |

### Internal Controls (SOX-lite for startups)

| Control | Purpose | Implementation |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| Segregation of duties | Prevent fraud | Different people approve and pay |
| Dual authorization | Catch errors | Two approvers for payments > threshold |
| Bank reconciliation | Detect discrepancies | Monthly, by someone other than the bookkeeper |
| Access controls | Limit exposure | Role-based access to financial systems |
| Expense approval | Control spending | Manager approval for all expenses |
| Vendor onboarding | Verify legitimacy | W-9 collection, payment verification |

## 409A Valuation

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| When needed | Before issuing stock options |
| Frequency | At least annually, or after material events |
| Material events | New funding round, significant revenue change, M&A |
| Safe harbor | Independent appraisal, <12 months old |
| Risk of non-compliance | Tax penalties on employees (20% + interest) |

---

## Key Regulatory Deadlines

| Item | Deadline | Penalty |
|------|----------|---------|
| Annual financial statements | Per investor/board requirements | Breach of agreements |
| Quarterly board financial package | Per board schedule | Governance failure |
| Sales tax filings | Monthly/quarterly by state | Penalties + interest |
| Payroll tax deposits | Semi-weekly or monthly | Trust fund penalties (personal liability) |
| 1099 filing | January 31 | $50-$280 per form |
| W-2 filing | January 31 | $50-$280 per form |
| 409A valuation | Before option grants | 20% tax penalty on employees |

---

## Notes

- Payroll tax penalties can be personal liability for officers — never miss
- Sales tax nexus is triggered by economic presence (post-Wayfair) — monitor
- 409A must be current before granting options — no backdating
- Always consult CPA for tax-specific decisions
