# Accounting Principles

## Cash vs Accrual

**Cash basis:** Record when money changes hands. Simpler. Common for small businesses.
**Accrual basis:** Record when earned/incurred, regardless of payment. GAAP standard. Required for larger companies.

Always confirm which basis the workspace uses before categorizing.

## Key principles

1. **Revenue recognition** — record revenue when earned, not when received (accrual)
2. **Matching principle** — match expenses to the revenue they generate
3. **Consistency** — use the same methods period to period
4. **Materiality** — don't obsess over immaterial amounts
5. **Conservatism** — when uncertain, choose the less favorable outcome

## SaaS-specific accounting

- **MRR:** Monthly Recurring Revenue — contracts normalized to monthly
- **ARR:** MRR × 12
- **Deferred revenue:** Cash received for service not yet delivered
- **Prepaid expenses:** Cash paid for service not yet received
- **CAC:** Total sales + marketing / new customers acquired
- **LTV:** ARPU × (1 / churn rate)
